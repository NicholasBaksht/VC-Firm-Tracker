Methodology & Sources

Rankings: firms are ordered by assets under management (AUM), highest to lowest. AUM figures are approximate — venture firms round these numbers in public statements, and totals shift as new funds are raised. Treat the ranking as a snapshot of relative scale, not a precise or constantly current figure.

Power Score: a 0–100 rating computed entirely from data already on this page — never opinion, never editorial judgment. It's a weighted formula: 35% Portfolio Breadth (how many tracked public exits, relative to the firm with the most), 35% Capital Scale (AUM, relative to the largest firm tracked), 15% Track Record (years active, relative to the longest-running firm), and 15% Verified Performance (average stock return since Jan 2, 2025, across holdings with a confirmed historical price on file). Firms without verified performance data yet receive a neutral score for that portion — never a penalty for a gap in our data collection, not their track record. The score recalculates automatically as more data is added.

Portfolio companies: each firm's listed holdings are early-stage investments that are publicly confirmed — either by the firm itself, by the company's own IPO/SEC filings, or by reputable financial reporting. Only companies that are now publicly traded are shown, since that's the only way to verify a real, checkable return.

Primary sources used to compile this data:

SEC Investment Adviser Public Disclosure ↗ Crunchbase ↗ PitchBook ↗ TechCrunch ↗ Fortune ↗

Each firm's own website (linked on its card above) was also used directly to confirm its founding year, headquarters, and stated investment focus.

How to use this: stock prices update automatically once a day. Founding details, AUM, and portfolio companies are updated manually — see the Methodology section above for how that data is sourced.
